01

Recommended Tech Stack

Given your goals — both iOS and Android, a real developer, bootstrapped budget — the best approach is a single codebase that runs on both platforms at once. This means writing the app once instead of twice, which cuts build time and cost significantly. Here's the stack I'd recommend:

Frontend / App

React Native (Expo)

One codebase, runs on both iOS and Android. Expo is the beginner-friendly wrapper that removes a lot of the painful setup. Your developer writes JavaScript/TypeScript — a widely known language, easy to hire for.

Free

Backend / Database

Supabase

Think of this as your app's brain and memory. It stores all your data (churches, users, prayer requests, messages), handles login/auth, and has a generous free tier that will easily cover MVP scale. No server to manage.

Free tier

Authentication

Supabase Auth

Built into Supabase. Handles email/password login, session management, and account security. No extra service needed. You can add phone/SMS auth later for extra verification.

Free

Real-time Messaging

Supabase Realtime

Also built into Supabase. Powers the Connect tab — messages appear instantly without refreshing. No need for a separate messaging service at MVP scale. Scales to a dedicated service (like Stream) later if needed.

Free tier

File / Image Storage

Supabase Storage

If users upload profile photos or documents. Also built into Supabase. 1GB free on the free tier — more than enough for MVP.

Free tier

Maps (Globe + Local)

Mapbox

Powers both the local church map and the globe view. Mapbox has a generous free tier (50,000 map loads/month) and is easier to customize than Google Maps — important for the RAG dot styling on countries.

Free tier

App Distribution

Expo EAS Build

Builds and submits your app to the App Store and Google Play. Expo handles the hard parts. Free tier available. You'll need Apple Developer ($99/yr) and Google Play ($25 one-time) accounts to publish.

Free tier

Encryption (Persecuted Church)

Supabase Row Level Security

Supabase has built-in row-level security — meaning you can restrict who sees what data at the database level. The Persecuted Church section's sensitive data is only readable by verified users. No extra service needed for MVP.

Free
Why this stack works for Replant Supabase + React Native (Expo) is one of the most popular beginner-friendly stacks right now. There is massive community support, thousands of free tutorials, and your developer will almost certainly know it. Everything on this list has a free tier that covers MVP scale — meaning you can launch and grow before spending a dollar on infrastructure.

03

User Flow

This is the journey every new user takes from first opening the app to being active in the network.

1

Download & Open

User downloads from App Store or Google Play. Splash screen loads with the Replant logo and two options: Create Account or Sign In.

2

Declaration of Faith

Before any form, the full affirmation is presented. User must tap "I Affirm This" to proceed. This is stored as a confirmed timestamp in the database. No affirmation = no account.

3

Create Account (Step 1 of 2) — Personal Info

Legal full name, email, password. This is private — never shown publicly. A verification email is sent.

4

Church Setup (Step 2 of 2)

Ministry/church name (required), church type dropdown, city/country, role, anonymity preference. Underground church selection disables location field and shows safety popup.

5

Enter the App — Home Tab

User lands on the Home tab. A banner at the top reminds them their church is pending verification (X days remaining). They can browse but certain actions (e.g. Persecuted Church submissions) may require verified status.

6

Register / Confirm Church

From the Church at My Location tab, user taps ✦ Register Your Church. Two-page form: church details + contact info (Page 1), then declaration of state + visibility preferences (Page 2). Replant team is notified via email.

7

Verification by Replant Team

A Replant team member contacts the leader via the email or phone they provided. Once confirmed, their account is marked verified in the database. The countdown stops. Full access is granted.

8

Active in the Network

User can now post prayer requests, respond to intercession, connect with leaders, submit heartcries (if persecuted), and appear on the local and global maps per their visibility settings.

APIs You'll Need

An API is simply a service your app talks to in order to get something done. Think of it like a phone call your app makes to another service. Here are the ones Replant needs and what they actually do in plain terms.

Supabase API

Free tier

What it does: This is your entire backend. Every time the app reads or saves data — a new prayer request, a user logging in, a church being registered — it's talking to Supabase. It also handles real-time messaging. Think of it as the engine behind everything. Your developer will use this constantly.

Mapbox API

Free up to 50k loads/month

What it does: Draws the maps. The local church map (Church at My Location) and the interactive globe (Church at Large) both run on Mapbox. It also lets you place custom colored dots (RAG status) on countries and zoom into regions. Think of it as the map drawing service.

Google Geocoding API

Free tier (then pay-as-you-go)

What it does: Converts a city name that a user types (e.g. "Loganville, Georgia") into exact coordinates (latitude/longitude) so the app knows where to place their church dot on the map. Think of it as the address-to-location translator. Free for MVP scale — you'd need thousands of daily lookups before paying anything.

Resend (or SendGrid) Email API

Free tier

What it does: Sends emails automatically — verification emails when someone signs up, and notifications to your Replant team email when a new church registers and needs verification. Think of it as your app's post office. Resend has a very generous free tier and is beginner-friendly. 100 emails/day free.

Expo Notifications API

Free

What it does: Not needed for MVP — but worth setting up early so it's ready. When you do add push notifications (e.g. urgent persecuted church alerts), this is what sends them to iOS and Android devices. Think of it as the tap-on-the-shoulder service. Built into Expo, completely free.
