The Road Ahead

The vision is clear.
The time is now.

There is an urgency on this. We are not waiting for everything to be perfect before we move — we are moving and trusting God to complete what He started.

Active · Moving now

Where we are. Where we're going.

This is not a distant dream. The foundation has already been laid — the vision, the brand, the wireframes, the technical plan. What follows is the path from here to a living, breathing network that connects the Body of Christ across the globe. Every step is deliberate. Every phase is Spirit-led.

Phase 02

Community Enrollment

Now — Urgent

In Progress

Before the app is built, the community must be gathered. This phase begins immediately — visiting local churches in the Loganville and Atlanta area, sharing the vision in person, and inviting leaders to register on the website. Web enrollment is the first real test of interest and the foundation for everything that follows.

In-person visits to local churches — Loganville, GA first
Share the vision, leave the card, invite enrollment

Web sign-up form capturing interest and contact details

Phase 03

Local Pilot

ASAP after Phase 2

Next

Once enough local churches have enrolled, we run a structured pilot within our community — testing how churches actually connect, what they need from the network, and where the gaps are. This is not a soft launch. This is the proving ground. What we learn here shapes the app.

Small group of verified local churches (5–15)
Manual coordination — prayer requests, needs, connections via web
Feedback sessions with pastors and leaders
Document what works, what doesn't, what's missing
Refine wireframes and feature priority based on real feedback

Why start with the community before the app?

Because the network is not the app — it is the people. The app is a tool. If we build the tool before the community, we build for an empty room.

By gathering churches first through web enrollment and in-person visits, we accomplish three things: we gauge real interest, we build trust before technology, and we ensure the first users of the app are already committed to the vision.

This is how movements are built. Not by launching a product, but by calling people together around a shared conviction.

1
Visit. Share. Invite.

In-person visits to local churches. Share the vision. Leave the card. Invite them to enroll at projectreplant.org.

2
Enroll via web.

Churches register their interest online. This builds our confirmed list and helps us understand who is ready to move.

3
Pilot together.

The first community of churches tests the network manually — prayer requests, needs, connections — before the app is built.

4
Build with feedback.

What we learn in the pilot shapes what we build. The app launches to a community that already knows what it's for.

What the app will do

The Replant app is already designed — every screen, every tab, every user flow. Development can begin the moment the community foundation is in place. Here's what leaders will have access to:

Home

Daily Scripture & Network Updates

A curated feed of encouragement, new church arrivals, and urgent needs across the network.

My Location

The Church at Your City

A live map of churches near you with status indicators, needs, and a direct line to connect.

Globe

The Church at Large

An interactive global view — zoom into any region, see the state of the church there, and offer support.

Set Apart

The Persecuted Church

A protected, encrypted section for churches facing active persecution. Handled with prayer and sobriety.

Prayer

Intercession Wall

Submit requests. Stand in the gap. Filter by urgency, region, and category. Add testimonies when God moves.

Connect

Direct Messaging

Secure, purposeful communication between churches and leaders — governed by a community covenant.
